Thermodynamics Experiments Using a Commercial Peltier Cell to Test the Efficiency of the Device for Wearable & On Body Applications.

I'm working on a wearable project and looking into possibilities to convert the heat generated by a scream into a low voltage power. As part of the reserach, I've spent the last months familiarizing with the concept of energy harvesting.

I ran two experiments in my kitchen using a Peltier module as a thermoelectric generator (TEG) and thermoelectric cooler (TEC). Below images and videos of my favourite accidents so far...


1. Experiment using aluminium heatsink (to the left), LED (1.8V diode), Peltier Module used as thermoelectric generator (TEG), ice & water.




2. Testing Seeback effect (discovered in 1821 by Thomas Johann Seebeck... of course); in thermoelectric generators, it takes a temperature difference and turns it into electrical power.




3. Experiment using a Peltier module as thermoelectric cooler (TEC1-12706) connected to a 3V battery.




4. Testing the Peltier effect (discovered in 1834 by Jean-Charles-Athanase Peltier): putting electricity into the Peltier module creates a temperature difference between the two sides (the top is hot, and the side in contact with the aluminum heatsink gets cold)
