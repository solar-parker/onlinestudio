I'm working on a wearable project and looking into possibilities to converts the heat waste generated by a scream into low voltage power. As part of the reserach, I've spent the last months familiarizing with the concept of energy harvesting.

I ran two experiments in my kitchen using the Peltier module as a thermoelectric generator (TEG) and thermoelectric cooler (TEC). Below images of my favourite accidents so far...


Experiment using aluminium heatsink (to the left), LED (1.8V diode), Peltier Module used as thermoelectric generator (TEG), ice & water


Testing Seeback effect (discovered in 1821 by Thomas Johann Seebeck... of course); in thermoelectric generators, it takes a temperature difference and turns it into electrical power


Experiment using a Peltier Module as thermoelectric cooler (TEC1-12706), aluminum heatsink, and 3V battery
