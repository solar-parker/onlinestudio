Maria Orciuoli’s work explores evolving forms of agency, resilience, and personal freedom within increasingly systematized realities.

Her visual language merges minimalist aesthetics with conceptual grit and the fantastic, conjuring bittersweet atmospheres punctuated by humor and surreal beauty. Working across video, film, drawing, installation, software interaction, and performance, she finds joy in transforming the everyday into sites of resistance or absurd inevitability.

In 4everfeed (2023–24), she drew from her experience as a social media practitioner to stage and document a three-month performance in which she enacted, under the fictional persona Alessia_3K, advertising scripts generated by TikTok’s Creative Assistant. The project culminated in a video installation capturing the irony and detachment of algorithmically optimized identities, exploring themes of digital labor, hyper-visibility, and the universal search for a better life. Raindrop Price Index (2023) is a meditation on hyperconnected solitude and the rationalist impulse to measure, animate, and control the natural world. The negotiation between individual actions and collective resonance in her interactive works, such as Ripple (2024), highlights the fragility of agency within both natural and human-made systems. Rather than rejecting these structures outright, Orciuoli’s works seek to expose their cracks—subverting, playing with, and navigating them on her own terms.

With a background in social sciences and electronic arts, she holds degrees from Luiss Guido Carli University, SRH Berlin, INSEEC Paris, and the University of Arts Linz. She has been a resident at the Digital Nature Group in Tsukuba, Japan, and Das Weisse Haus' dwhX.space in Vienna. Her work has been exhibited at Ars Electronica Center, Krupa Art Foundation, and The Wrong Biennale, among others.

In a parallel universe, she runs KO-HUM, a communication agency amplifying voices across music, contemporary art, literature, and new media—an act of mediation between creative freedom and the very systems that seek to contain it.

Much like the subjects of her inquiry, Orciuoli’s practice remains in flux. She currently moves between Linz, Berlin, and her native Italy.
