Maria Orciuoli’s work explores evolving forms of agency, resilience, and personal freedom within increasingly systematized realities.

Her visual language merges conceptual grit and the fantastic, conjuring bittersweet atmospheres punctuated by humor and surreal beauty. Working across video, film, drawing, installation, software programming, and performance, she transforms the everyday into sites of resistance or absurd inevitability, moving seamlessly between elaborating fictions and a research-based practice.

Orciuoli's films and video works often center around people who cross her path, relatives and versions of herself. 4everfeed (2023–24) is a conceptual video reel featuring the artist as an AI-generated influencer. Based on a three-month performance in which she enacted advertising scripts generated by TikTok’s Creative Assistant, 4everfeed traces the evolution of selfhood from human presence to digital spectacle. Through a fusion of real-time AR filters, live app effects, and generative transformations, the work is imbued with algorithmically-infused irony, capturing how platform labor shapes people’s lives, the urge for optimization, and the search for a better life.

The negotiation between individual actions and collective resonance in Ripple (2024), draws inspiration from everyday moments of connection—how people unconsciously adjust their movements in a crowded street or how a conversation shifts the mood of a room—transforming depth and distance into waves of light that propagate across the installation space.

Raindrop (2023) transforms global rainfall measurements into conduits for personal memory. Conceived in a period of hyperconnected solitude—between screens and the glass of a dormitory window in Japan at the onset of the rainy season—the work transforms climate data into audio-visual animations displayed on a glowing screen, taking the rationalist impulse to quantify and control the natural world into the realm of subjective experience.

With a background in social sciences and electronic arts, Orciuoli has been a resident at the Digital Nature Group in Tsukuba, Japan, and Das Weisse Haus' dwhX.space in Vienna. Her work has been exhibited at Ars Electronica Center, Krupa Art Foundation, and The Wrong Biennale, among others.

Somewhere between critique and complicity, she also runs KO-HUM, a communication agency amplifying voices across music, art, and literature—an act of mediation between creative freedom and the very systems that seek to contain it.

Much like the subjects of her inquiry, Orciuoli’s practice remains in flux. She currently moves between Linz, Berlin, and her native Italy.
